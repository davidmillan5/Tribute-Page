Frank Herbert

The mystery of life isn't a problem to solve, but a reality to experience.

Here I present some of the most important moments in the life of a man who left too soon:

  • 1920 - Born in Tacoma, Washington.
  • 1938 - He ran away from home to live with an aunt and uncle in Salem, Oregon.
  • 1939 - He enrolled in high school at Salem High School (now North Salem High School), where he graduated.
  • 1940 - He worked for the Oregon Statesman newspaper (now Statesman Journal) in a variety of positions, including photographer.
  • 1941 - Herbert married Flora Lillian Parkinson in San Pedro, California.
  • 1942 - After the U.S. entry into World War II, he served in the Navy's Seabees for six months as a photographer, but suffered an accidental head injury and was given a medical discharge.
  • 1943 - Divorced from Flora Lillian Parkinson.
  • 1946 - Attended the University of Washington, where he met Beverly Ann Stuart at a creative writing class.
  • 1946 - He married Beverly Ann Stuart in Seattle, Washington on June 20.
  • 1949 - Herbert and his wife moved to California to work on the Santa Rosa Press-Democrat.
  • 1952 - Herbert's first science fiction story, "Looking for Something", was published in the April.
  • 1955 - His career as a novelist began with the serial publication of Under Pressure in Astounding.
  • 1965 - The novel Dune was published.
  • 1965 - Won the Nebula Award for Best Novel.
  • 1966 - Won the Hugo Award.
  • 1969 - He became the Seattle Post-Intelligencer's education writer.
  • 1969 - The novel Dune Messiah was published.
  • 1972 - Retired from newspaper writing and become a full-time fiction writer.
  • 1976 - The novel Children of Dune was published.
  • 1981 - The novel God Emperor of Dune was published.
  • 1984 - The novel Heretics of Dune was published.
  • 1985- The novel Chapterhouse: Dune was published.
  • 1986 - He died of a massive pulmonary embolism while recovering from surgery for pancreatic cancer on February 11, in Madison, Wisconsin, age 65.
